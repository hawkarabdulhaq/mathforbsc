From number line → plane → space

Ordered pairs and triples as coordinates

Use set products to build the plane (A × A) and space ((A × A) × A) from a number line A.

English EN Magyar HU
In coordinate geometry, points of the plane are represented by ordered pairs of numbers. A koordináta‑geometriában a sík pontjait rendezett számpárokkal jellemzik.
Points of space are represented by ordered triples of numbers. A tér pontjait rendezett számhármasokkal jellemzik.
If A = the set of points on the number line, Ha A = a számegyenes pontjainak halmaza,
Then (A × A) = the set of all points in the plane. Akkor (A × A) = a sík pontjainak halmaza.
And (A × A) × A = the set of all points in space. És (A × A) × A = a tér pontjainak halmaza.
Visual intuition — A, A × A, (A × A) × A
Simple sketches: number line, plane coordinates, 3D coordinates.

Note: In standard analytic geometry, A is usually the set of real numbers ℝ, so the plane is ℝ × ℝ and space is ℝ × ℝ × ℝ.
